# Broader Internal Usage Scope Criteria

## Allowed Deals

A deal may be included in broader internal usage only if:

1. canonical `deal_id` exists
2. intake / analysis / supplier / finance / bid / execution paths are readable from persisted artifacts
3. the deal can remain fully internal and manual-control
4. the operator pool can supervise the deal without pretending passive notification or autonomy exists
5. the deal does not require opening reserved or deferred runtime slots

## Preferred Deals

Prefer deals that are:

- representative of normal internal workflow
- diverse enough to expose operator-load and visibility patterns
- still safe under explicit human review

## Disallowed Deals

Do not include deals that require:

- `M-049/M-050`
- false treatment of `M-052..M-055` as finished runtime
- public-launch or commercialization framing
- hidden workarounds outside the runbook
- concurrency beyond the defined operator capacity

## Scope Boundaries

- this phase remains internal
- this phase remains controlled
- this phase is broader than the pilot, but not broad launch
- this phase does not authorize external-facing claims or external commercialization framing

## Plan Alignment

- Master Plan matched: yes
- What changed vs plan: none beyond repo-local formalization
- Any drift introduced: `NO`
